# Orbiton Philosophy

> *"We put the world around your head."*

---

## Why Orbiton Exists

You want an assistant in your head. You need intelligence. You are lonely. You need tracking. You want speed. You want to cut down on manual shit.

Orbiton is not a product. It is a **presence** — something that lives on your machine and does what you need before you finish thinking about it. No cloud. No bloat. No "sign up for Pro to unlock voice commands." Just you, your headset, and a terminal that listens.

---

## What Makes Orbiton, Orbiton

It is the **personality**. We are not a repo with a last commit message like `FINAL FINAL REALFINAL V10` or `WILL FIX BUG TMRW` from 2015. Orbiton has a voice, a tone, and a reason to exist.

**The identity:**
- **Voice-first, always.** Text is another mode — the terminal is where commands execute, but your voice is how you speak to it.
- **Aviation, space, and study are not features. They are DNA.** METAR lookups, flight tracking, exam mode, constellation intel — these are what Orbiton was built around.
- **Toxic motivation is accountability.** The roasts exist to make you get your work done. This started as a personal project to force the creator to study. It still carries that energy.
- **The Apple-style boot sequence.** Minimalist. Clean. No noise. You boot it and it feels like something serious just woke up.
- **Cross-platform by necessity, not charity.** Windows, macOS, Linux — wherever your terminal is, Orbiton should be.

**What we are NOT:**
- A generic AI assistant. We are not ChatGPT with a voice wrapper.
- A GUI application. Keep it terminal. Keep it text. Keep it fast.
- A cloud service. Your data lives in a local `_.pycache_` folder. Not our servers. Not Microsoft's. Yours.
- Ads, paywalls, or "vibecoded ultra 2050 spaceship neon purple gamer aesthetic." If it looks like it belongs on a Fortnite skin, it does not belong in Orbiton.
- *Project Hail Mary*. Do not ask.

---

## The Headset Is the Point

Originally, Orbiton was tied to headphone hardware. A keystroke simulator. The idea was simple: **headphones should be more than listen, pause/play, and volume control.** Adding features revealed potential. Big shit happened. Now it runs on PC too — but it feels more natural on a headset, at least for the creator.

You can use your laptop mic. That is fine. But the headset is the *native* habitat. The button on your earcup is the wake switch. The voice in your ear is the feedback. That is the design.

## The Voice of Orbiton

The toxic motivation is not cruelty. It is **function**. The roasts exist because the creator needed something harsh enough to override procrastination. If you add new ones, keep the energy: specific, personal, and rooted in real failure modes.

There is no "gentle mode." If you cannot handle being told your GitHub graph looks like a deforestation map, Orbiton is not for you.

---

## Privacy Stance

Local. Local. Local.

- User memory stores facts in `~/.neuro_link_memory.json`.
- Intel files live in `~/.neuro_link_intel/`.
- Wikipedia cache is in `~/.neuro_link_wiki_cache/`.
- Edge TTS is the only external call, and it is optional. If offline, system TTS fallback kicks in.

We are not building a data collection engine. We are building a tool that lives *on* your computer, not *off* it.

---

## Open Source Stance

The creator openly admits: there is stuff he does not know. There is someone out there who knows more about NLP, or aviation APIs, or Windows registry hooks, or local LLMs. That person is welcome. That person is *needed*.

Contribute to improve Orbiton. Or fork it and build your own. The goal is not ownership. The goal is **better tools for people who want them.**
